the term vanilla CSS os used to describe plain old ‘classic’ CSS that doesn’t rely on other plugins or frameworks or libraries. Vanilla CSS is extremely powerful on its own and provides you with a multitude of styling tools.

I cannot begin to emphasise the importance of learning vanilla CSS if you are looking to do frontend work. Yes there are a lot of amazing libraries like Bootsrap that enable you to do things much faster and not have to worry about the intricate details of positioning and styling elements. But in my personal opinion, knowing “what’s going on under the hood” is an extremely valuable tool to have.

Most features that libraries provide you can all boil down to vanilla HTML, CSS and Javascript. knowing these languages in their pure form and on a fundamental level gives a lot of power in navigating you codebase and making things look and work how you want them to.

Moreover, on the debugging front, the need for knowing how and why things work is probably the only fundamental truth. What I mean by that is that, logically speaking, you cannot fix something when you have no effing idea how and why that thing works.
